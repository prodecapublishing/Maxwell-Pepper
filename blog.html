About This Book

ACT vocabulary flashcards have a retention problem: students read the definition, feel like they know the word, and then cannot recall it under pressure on test day. Maxwell Pepper's ACT Vocabulary: The Cipher Conspiracy solves this by embedding 200+ high-frequency ACT words inside a gripping detective thriller. When you encounter 'obfuscate' in a tense scene where a character deliberately misleads a detective, your brain builds a rich memory around it — one that survives the pressure of the actual ACT.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this only for ACT or does it help with SAT too?
Primarily ACT-focused, but the word lists have substantial overlap with SAT vocabulary. Most words in this guide appear with high frequency on both exams.
How does learning vocab through a story actually work?
Research in cognitive science shows that words learned in context — especially emotionally engaging context — are retained far better than words learned from lists. Narrative memory is one of the most durable forms of learning.
Can I read this casually or is it structured study?
Both. The story is designed to be genuinely entertaining, not just a vocabulary delivery vehicle. A word glossary and review sections let you study formally, or you can read the thriller and absorb the words naturally.
Does this cover all the vocabulary on the ACT?
The ACT does not publish an official vocabulary list. This guide covers 200+ words that appear with high frequency across recent ACT exams, giving you strong coverage of the most commonly tested vocabulary.
What ACT score improvement can I expect from improving vocabulary?
Vocabulary directly impacts ACT English and Reading scores. Strong vocabulary reduces time spent on comprehension questions and improves accuracy on rhetoric questions. Most students see measurable improvement in their verbal scores after completing this guide.
