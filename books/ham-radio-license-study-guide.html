Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a math or electronics background? +
No. The Technician exam has some basic electronics, but nothing that requires prior knowledge. The book explains all concepts from scratch using plain language and practical analogies. If you can understand why a light switch works, you're ready.
Is this for the Technician license only, or General too? +
This guide is focused on the Technician class license — the entry-level FCC amateur radio license. It gives you the foundation to later pursue General and Extra class licenses with more advanced study materials.
How long does it take to prepare for the ham radio exam? +
Most candidates are ready in 1–3 weeks studying 30–60 minutes per day. The exam draws from a published 426-question pool, so knowing the pool well is the core study strategy.
Is the question pool the same everywhere in the US? +
Yes. The Technician question pool is standardized nationwide by the NCVEC. All 35 exam questions are drawn from the published pool, which is updated every 4 years. This guide covers the current question pool.
